About

Where the plan meets the people, systems, and habits on the ground.

I work at the intersection of strategy, operations, and technology. Over the past ten years, across tech, fintech, Web3, gaming, and retail, I've led transformation work spanning operating model design, organisational development, ERP and CRM implementation, process optimisation, and change management.

What I keep coming back to is the moment a plan made in a meeting room actually lands on the floor, with the teams running it, the tools they're stuck using, and the habits they've built up over years. That's usually where things either click into place or quietly fall apart, and it's the part most people underestimate.

Most recently, this has meant redesigning operating models and decision-making structures for a retail technology business moving from founder-led decisions to a structure built to scale.

Based in Singapore, and always happy to talk to people working in transformation, operations, or organisational growth.

How I Work

Six steps I come back to on every project.

This is roughly the rhythm, whether it's one workflow or a whole operating model.

01

Understand

Before I touch anything, I spend time watching how things get done day to day. The org chart and the SOP rarely tell the full story.

02

Map

I lay out the full process, every step, system, and handoff, so everyone involved is looking at the same picture I am.

03

Design

I look for the pattern behind the problem. More often than not, the structure sitting around a step is what's causing the trouble.

04

Prioritise

I decide what to fix first based on what will actually move the needle, even when it's not the thing everyone's been complaining about.

05

Implement

I stay close to the rollout. A plan only really counts once it's working in practice.

06

Adopt & Sustain

This is the part most people skip. I spend time helping people let go of old habits, so the new way sticks.

A Few Examples

What this has looked like in practice.

Process Redesign

Five steps, one flow

20%+ fasterand contributed to business growth

Customers had several ways into the same process, the systems behind it were slow, and both customers and frontline staff were stuck working through five separate steps for what should have been one transaction. I worked with the team to map the journey end to end, and the underlying problem turned out to be the number of steps itself, not any single broken step.

The harder part was getting people to let go of habits they'd built over years, so we brought the team in early and showed them why the new way made their work easier, not harder. Five steps became one, frontline operating time dropped by more than 20%, and the redesign contributed to the business's broader growth.

System Migration

One system, five markets

10-min response78% CSAT across 5 markets

Support for over 500,000 users across China, Taiwan, and Europe ran through a ticketing system where routing and tagging hadn't kept up with how the teams worked. Tickets often landed with the wrong team, in the wrong language, making it hard to track issues or compare performance across regions.

I worked on the migration to a new system, redesigning the routing logic and tagging structure so tickets reached the right team regardless of language, and standardising reporting so performance could finally be compared across markets. First response time came down to around 10 minutes, with CSAT reaching 78% across the multilingual operation.

Crisis Response

Holding the line during a spike

3,000+ usersnear-zero escalations

A promotion brought in over 3,000 users at once, all wanting the same thing, inside a regulated financial environment where every step had to hold up to compliance. I coordinated across customer service, compliance, and operations in real time, working out what needed attention first while keeping everything within audit requirements.

We got through it with close to zero escalations, kept the service running, and didn't compromise on compliance.
